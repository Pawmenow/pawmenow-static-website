When do Corgis stop growing?

Corgis, like all dogs, go through different growth stages. It's essential to know when your Corgi will reach its full size to ensure they receive the proper care and nutrition during their development. Generally, Corgis stop growing in height between 9 and 12 months of age. However, their growth might not be entirely finished until they are around 18 months old. Keep in mind that individual dogs may have slight variations in their growth patterns.

Corgi Growth Chart

To give you a better idea of what to expect as your Corgi pup grows, here's a general growth chart:

  1. Newborn to 3 Months: During this phase, Corgi puppies experience rapid growth. They will double their birth weight within the first week and continue to gain weight steadily.
  2. 3 to 6 Months: This is the time when Corgi puppies start developing their distinctive appearance, including their short legs and long bodies. They'll grow rapidly during these months, both in height and weight.
  3. 6 to 12 Months: Corgis enter their adolescence phase, and their growth rate begins to slow down. You'll notice their height and body proportions start to stabilize during this period.
  4. 12 to 18 Months: Corgis are close to reaching their full size by this age. Some may continue to fill out a bit and gain muscle mass, but their height should remain relatively consistent.

When do Corgi ears stop growing?

Corgis are known for their expressive, upright ears, which add to their charming appearance. But when do these adorable ears stop growing? Most Corgis' ears reach their full size by the time they're around six months old. However, the exact timing can vary from one dog to another. Some Corgis might have fully erect ears earlier, while others might take a little longer. It's worth noting that Corgi ears might wiggle or flop during their early months, but they generally stand upright as they mature.

At what age are corgis full-grown?

Corgis are considered full-grown when they reach their adult height and have stopped growing in length. Typically, Corgis reach their full height between 9 and 12 months of age, with their growth completing around 18 months. By this age, they will have achieved their mature appearance, including their signature low, elongated bodies and sturdy legs.

The average lifespan of a Corgi is around 12 to 13 years, but many factors can influence their health and longevity:

  • Proper nutrition is crucial throughout their life. Ensure you feed them a balanced diet appropriate for their age and activity level.
