Dogs With Hair Not Fur: 5 Breeds That You'll Adore

Are you a dog lover but suffer from allergies? Dogs with hair instead of fur may be the solution you need. These hypoallergenic breeds shed less and produce less dander, making them ideal pets for people with allergies. ü§é

For many people, dogs are more than just pets - they're family. However, for those with allergies, having a dog in the house can be a challenge. Dogs with hair instead of fur can help in that situation. These breeds are excellent choices for allergy people since they produce less dander and shed less. In plus, hypoallergenic dogs don't smell.

In this article, we'll take a closer look at five breeds of dogs with hair instead of fur that you're sure to adore.

What breed of dog has hair instead of fur?

If you're looking for a hypoallergenic dog, here are five breeds with hair instead of fur:

  1. Poodle
  2. Bichon Frise
  3. Yorkshire Terrier
  4. Portuguese Water Dog
  5. Afghan Hound

1. Poodle - The Elegant and Intelligent Companion

Poodles are a breed of dog that come in different sizes, are very clever, and have curly, hypoallergenic hair.

2. Bichon Frise - The Fluffy and Cheerful Friend

Bichon Frises are a small breed of dog with a soft and fluffy coat of hair. Like Poodles, they have hair instead of fur, which makes them hypoallergenic. However, their coat requires regular grooming to prevent tangling and matting. Bichon Frises are cheerful, friendly, and love to be around people.

3. Yorkshire Terrier - The Spunky and Loyal Companion

Yorkshire Terriers are tiny dogs with a silky coat of hair that requires regular grooming. Like the other breeds on this list, they have hair instead of fur, which makes them a good option for people with allergies. The energetic temperament of Yorkshire Terriers are well recognized, and they can make wonderful companion pets.

4. Portuguese Water Dog: The Athletic and Loyal Worker

The medium-sized Portuguese Water Dog is a breed of dog with a curly, non-shedding coat of hair. They are generally regarded as hypoallergenic, although their coat needs frequent grooming to prevent matting. These dogs are renowned for their intelligence and athleticism since they were originally raised to work alongside fisherman.

5. Afghan Hound - Known for their grace and beauty

Afghan Hounds are a large breed of dog with a long and silky coat of hair. They have hair instead of fur, which means their coat requires regular grooming to prevent tangling and matting. Afghan Hounds are known for their grace and beauty, and they were originally bred for hunting in Afghanistan.

Why do some dogs have hair not fur?

Hair and fur differ from one another in terms of texture and purpose. Fur is protective and grows more quickly, but hair grows slower and offers warmth. Due to genetic variations, certain dog breeds have hair rather than fur.
