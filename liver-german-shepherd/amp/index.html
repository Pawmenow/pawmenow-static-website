Liver German Shepherds [Full Brown]: Everything You Need to Know

Pawmenow - 06 Mar 2023

There is a good reason why German Shepherds are one of the most well-liked dog breeds worldwide. They make excellent companions for families, and members of the police enforcement and military because of their devotion, intelligence, and protective temperament. The full brown German Shepherd is a rare color variety of the breed; the majority are black and tan or sable.

🤎 Full Brown German Shepherd Appearance

A full-brown German Shepherd has a whole brown coat. While some German Shepherds' coats may be colored brown, a full-brown German Shepherd has a coat that is completely brown from head to toe. A recessive gene that is uncommon in the breed is the reason of this uncommon color difference.

How rare are liver German Shepherds?

"Liver" German Shepherds, also known as chocolate or brown, are considered to be a rare color variation of the breed. Given that the black and tan, sable, and solid black colors of German Shepherds are more prevalent, the liver color is a recessive gene that is less common.

German Shepherds with liver coloring have brown or chocolate-colored coats and pink or liver-colored lips and noses. Their eyes are light or amber. But, it's crucial to remember that even though liver German Shepherds are uncommon, they are not a distinct breed and can still display the same temperamental traits and health problems as other German Shepherds.

As a result of their rarity, some breeders may charge a premium for liver-colored German Shepherds, but it's essential to do careful research and select a reputable breeder who puts the health and welfare of their dogs' over their coat color. In addition, regardless of the color of their coat, there are lots of German Shepherds in shelters and rescues who need homes.

Physical Characteristics

A full-brown German Shepherd shares many of the same physical traits as a standard German Shepherd. They are a big breed, weighing between 50 and 90 pounds and towering between 22 and 26 inches tall at the shoulder. They are well-built, with straight backs and powerful legs. They have long, pointed snouts and upright ears.

The color of their coat is the only physical distinction between a typical German Shepherd and a full-brown German Shepherd. The full brown German Shepherd has a solid brown coat that ranges in shade from a light tan to a dark chocolate brown.

Personality and Temperament

Full brown German Shepherds have the same personality and temperament as classic German Shepherds.

They are wonderful family pets since they are devoted, watchful, and smart dogs. They are frequently employed as police and military dogs because of their reputation for bravery and fearlessness.

Training and Exercise

German Shepherds are extremely smart and simple to train. They respond favorably to positive reinforcement training techniques, and they like mental and physical challenges.

These dogs must get some exercise every day, such as walking, running, and playing. To keep their minds active, they also require mental stimulation from puzzle toys or training exercises.

Health Issues

Full brown German Shepherds could be vulnerable to specific health problems, just like any purebred dogs. Hip dysplasia, elbow dysplasia, bloat, and allergies are a few of the most prevalent health issues in German Shepherds. It's crucial to get a complete brown German Shepherd from a reputable breeder who checks their breeding dogs for health issues.

Conclusion

German Shepherds that are fully brown are a rare and stunning color variation of the original German Shepherd breed. They are equally devoted and guarding as their black and tan counterparts, and they make wonderful family pets. If you're thinking about getting a full brown German Shepherd, do your research and choose a reputable breeder who puts their dogs' health and wellbeing first.
