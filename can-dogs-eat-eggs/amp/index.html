Are Eggs Safe For Dogs?

The answer is: YES! Eggs are safe for dogs.

Eggs are a great source of nutrition for your dog because they contain a high level of protein, vitamins (A, D, E, K, B1, B6, B12), fatty acids, and necessary minerals that are healthy for your dog.

Can dogs eat raw eggs?

The answer is: NOT REALLY. Raw eggs aren't recommended for dogs because some eggs can contain Salmonella. The risk of contamination is much higher for uncooked eggs. So it's recommended that dogs must consume pasteurized or fully cooked eggs.

If your dog gets infected by Salmonella it may infect people and other pets around them. ¬†The symptoms of infections can be fever, diarrhea, and stomach cramps.

So, the main idea: don't risk giving your dog raw eggs when cooked eggs take just a few minutes.

Can dogs eat cooked eggs?

YES! Dogs can eat cooked eggs and it is perfectly safe for them.
If you cook eggs for your dog, the easiest way is to boil them. If you choose this option, is simpler because it isn't that messy and you don't have to add oil.

As a safe answer, I would suggest talking to its vet. A vet knows better if your dog can eat eggs or not.

Eggs contain lots of needed nutrients for dogs, but eggs aren't the main source of protein for your dog. If your dog eats more than one egg per day it can lead to weight gain because of the calories consumed.

Also, you can use the egg as a special treat for your dog. Eggs are also tasty treats that can be used for pups.
