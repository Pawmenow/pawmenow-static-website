When it comes to sun protection, humans frequently take precautions. Should we also protect our pets in the same way? Do dogs ever get sunburns?

Well, as we get sunburned, so do dogs can. But should dogs need sunscreen? There are pretty much questions on this subject. In this article, I'm going to answer all of them.

A sure thing we've already agreed on is that dogs can get sunburned. That's why there are plenty of sun protection products on the market that is made especially for dogs.

What kind of sunscreen is safe for dogs? Can I use human sunscreen on my dog?

One sure thing to remember is that sunscreen for humans is to be avoided on dogs.

No matter how tempting it may seem to take one bottle of sunscreen for the entire family, human sunscreen shouldn't be used on dogs.

Most sunscreens contain zinc oxide or titanium oxide, which can be harmful in excessive amounts even if some varieties may be okay for occasional usage. If your dog tasted a lot, it would be a serious issue.

But what about sunscreen for kids? Many dog owners thought that baby sunscreen may be good for dogs due to its nontoxic ingredients. Continue to scroll to find out more.

What dogs really need sunscreen?
Sun protection is helpful for all dogs, but some require it more than others. Dogs with short or thin coats, as well as those with white fur and pink skin, are more sensitive to the sun, so they really need to be protected with pet-friendly sunscreen.

Dog sunburn can happen to any breed, even those with darker skin tones or thicker coats. For this reason, along with many others, it's crucial to give your dog a shaded area to rest and monitor the amount of time they spend in the sun, just like you would for you.

An overview of warning signs that your dog may get sunburn is as follows:

  • Pink skin & White fur (or both)
  • Light-colored nose, eyelids, & ears
  • No fur at all, thin coats, or thinly dispersed
  • Major shedding or hair loss
  • Dermatitis and other persistent skin problems
  • Current sunburns
  • Healing wounds or surgical incision locations that have been shaved
