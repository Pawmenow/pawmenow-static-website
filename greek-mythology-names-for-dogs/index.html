Greek Gods Names for Dogs: Best 33 Inspired From Mythology

Naming your dog isn't always an easy task because you want a great name that fits your dog's unique personality and handsome look. When is about name inspiration, Greek Mythology is a great source from where you can get inspired.

From gods and goddesses to esteemed heroes, there are plenty of mythic options to choose from, to be as unique as your dog is.

Mythical Greek names often have a great story behind them. Like the characters they represent, the mythical names evoke the human experience going back in time. Also, an interesting thing is that "dog" spelled backward is "god". Perhaps it's because our canine friends have always been part of human mythology about gods and goddesses.

As gods and goddesses, strength and bravery are present in our dogs, too. That's why Greek mythology names may be a good idea when you want to choose the best name for your dog.

Greek god names for male dogs

The following male characters of Greek mythology are some of the most known figures of all time. Choose between these popular Greek dog names:

  • Apollo - the god of poetry, prophecy, archery, music, medicine, and later, the god of the sun
  • Ares - the god of war and courage
  • Ajax - the strong and brave hero of the Trojan War
  • Argus - the giant watchful guardian with 100 eyes on his body that never fell asleep
  • Brontes - one of the Cyclopes of Greek mythology, whose name means 'thunder'
  • Damon & Pythias - for owners with two male pups (the representation of trust and loyalty) - ¬†in Greek legend, a celebrated pair of friends who came to signify the willingness to sacrifice oneself for the sake of a friend
  • Evander - the Greek cultural hero that introduced some of the blessings of civilization, including writing
  • Eros - the god of love and fertility
  • Helios - the god of the sun
  • Hermes - the most clever of the Olympian gods with strength, durability, stamina, agility, and reflexes
  • Jason - the brave leader of the Argonauts. He did everything he could to get his kingdom back
  • Linos - the leader of lyric song, a great musician and the personification of lamentation
  • Morpheus - god of sleep and dreams that appears in dreams in human form
  • Poseidon - the god of the sea and of water generally, earthquakes, and horses
  • Pan - god of the wild
  • Perseus - the destroyer of the Gorgon Medusa and the rescuer of Andromeda from a sea monster
  • Theseus - the mythical king and founder-hero of Athens
  • Zeus - the sky and thunder god in ancient Greek religion, which rules over the gods of Olympus (most popular name)

Greek goddess names for dogs

Mythology is filled with strong women, too. Women that were competing against their male counterparts.

Naming your female dog after a mythical Greek goddess or great character is a great way to solemnize her personality.

  • Aphrodite - the goddess of beauty and love
  • Artemis - the goddess of the wild animals, hunt, vegetation and child birth
  • Aura - the goddess of the breeze, fresh and air in the morning
  • Calliope - the muse who leads over eloquence and epic poetry
  • Clio - the muse of history, song and dance
  • Daphne - a minor figure in Greek mythology, a nymph, associated with fountains, wells, springs, streams, brooks
  • Echo - a magical nymph
  • Helen (of Troy) - the most beautiful woman of Greece (in Greek legend), daughter of Zeus and the indirect cause of the Trojan War
  • Hera - the goddess of women, marriage, family and childbirth
  • Iris - the personification of the rainbow and a messenger of the gods
  • Nyx - the goddess of the night
  • Pandora - the first woman
  • Penelope - devotion and fidelity
  • Rhea - the goddess of fertility and motherhood
  • Selene - the goddess of the moon
