Can Dogs Eat McFlurry? Xylitol Free, But Is it Safe or Not?

Pawmenow

07 Mar 2023 2 min

McFlurry can be a delicious dessert for us, humans. For dogs, the situation is different.

Market or fast-food ice cream is generally hazardous to dogs since it contains sugar and other preservatives that dogs should not consume.

Despite the fact that a recent study found that McDonald's ice cream does not contain xylitol, McFlury is not a healthy option for your dog. Let's find out why!

Can dogs eat McFlurry?

The answer is no. McFlurry shouldn't be consumed by dogs. Depending on how sensitive your dog is, eating McFlurry may induce stomach pain or worse. Ice cream can cause gas, bloating, constipation, diarrhea, and vomiting in your furry friend.

What happens if my dog ate McFlurry and he/she feels bad?

If your dog ate more McFlurry than it should and is showing symptoms such as vomiting, gas, lethargy, bloating, constipation, or diarrhea, please take him/her to the vet as soon as possible.

In general, it is best to avoid giving your pet any type of McDonald's meal because they are not like us and can be harmful to them.
