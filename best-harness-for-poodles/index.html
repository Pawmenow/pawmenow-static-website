9 Best Harness for Poodles [Toy, Miniature, Standard]

Pawmenow

10 Jan 2023 5 min

Whether you're looking to take your Poodle for a walk, on a hike, or just out running errands, it's important to have the right harness to keep them safe and comfortable. In this article, we'll explore some of the best harnesses available for Poodles, so you can choose the perfect one for your furry friend.

Do Poodles need a harness?

Poodles, like all dogs, do not technically "need" a harness, but it can be beneficial for both the dog and the owner in many cases.

Poodles are prone to tracheal collapse, a condition that makes it difficult for them to breathe. Collars can put pressure on a dog's neck, which can worsen the condition. Harness distributes pressure evenly across the chest and shoulders, helping to prevent choking and other injuries, and maybe a more suitable option for Poodles.

Additionally, a harness can provide greater control and flexibility when walking your Poodle.

Many harnesses have two leash attachment points, one on the chest and one on the back, which can help to prevent pulling and make it easier to control your dog during walks. Harness with a handle in the back also allows you to guide your dog during hikes or runs, or even assist them in difficult terrain.

Furthermore, for service dogs, or even for Poodles that are well-behaved, a harness is often the preferred option as it provides a more secure fit and it doesn't put pressure on the dog's neck.

What size harness does a toy poodle need?

The size of the harness your Toy Poodle will need will depend on the specific measurements of your dog. It is important to properly measure your dog and refer to the size chart provided by the manufacturer when selecting a harness.

Typically, Toy Poodles weigh between 4-6 pounds and can have a girth measurement (the widest part of the chest) of around 13-15 inches. But just like any other dog, each Toy Poodle may have unique measurements, that's why is always recommended to measure your dog to ensure you get the perfect fit.

It's also important to make sure that the harness you choose is adjustable so that you can get a custom fit for your dog. A harness that is too loose or too tight will not be as effective in preventing pulling or providing support. Some harnesses come with adjustable straps on the chest and belly, and it's important to adjust them to snugly fit around your dog.

What size harness for a Standard Poodle?

The size of the harness for a Standard Poodle will depend on the specific measurements of your dog. Standard Poodles can vary greatly in size, with a typical weight range between 45-70 pounds and girth measurement (the widest part of the chest) between 24-30 inches, depending on the individual dog.

It's important to measure your dog and refer to the size chart provided by the manufacturer when selecting a harness.

Please remember to always supervise and keep an eye on your dog while using any type of equipment and make sure you are properly educated and aware of the laws and rules in your area. Be safe and enjoy your time with your furry friend!

Is a collar or harness better for a Poodle?

Both collars and harnesses have their own set of benefits and drawbacks, and the best option for your poodle will depend on your specific needs and the behavior of your dog.

Collars are typically the more traditional and common choice for dogs, and they're easy to put on and take off. They're also usually less expensive than harnesses. However, collars can put pressure on a dog's neck, which can be especially problematic for dogs that pull on the leash or have respiratory issues. This is particularly true of Poodles, which are prone to tracheal collapse, a condition that makes it difficult for them to breathe.

A harness, on the other hand, distributes pressure evenly across the chest and shoulders, which helps to prevent choking or other injuries. This is generally a better option for poodles, as it reduces the risk of tracheal collapse, and it doesn't put pressure on the dog's neck. Additionally, some harnesses have a handle in the back to help control the dog, which can be useful during hikes or runs.

It's also worth noting that depending on the local laws and rules in your area, using a harness for service animals might be mandatory, for example.

In general, a harness is a better option for poodles, especially for those that pull or have respiratory issues. It's important to make sure that the harness you choose is properly fitted to your dog, and that you spend some time getting your poodle accustomed to wearing it before going out for a walk or hike. It is always recommended to consult with a professional trainer or veterinarian before making any final decision.
